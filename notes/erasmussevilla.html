My Erasmus experience in Sevilla

Posted on July 15, 2021 by Eduard Frankford
Sevilla Erasmus

In September 2020 I went to Sevilla to study at the Universidad de Sevilla. Therefore, I thought this article might help anyone who is thinking about doing the same. Spoler alert: It was an amazing experience and I can definitely recommend you to do it as well.

Travel preparations:

To be honest, I didn't prepare much for my Erasmus until about a month before my departure. But when panic spread two weeks before departure, I copied all my personal documents which was good because in the Centro international you need a copy of your passport and ecard and passport photos (in physical form)!). In addition, I brought a copy of my health insurance and informed my bank about my stay. Furthermore, I received an insurance confirmation from the ÖH. (which would not have been necessary).

In advance it is very helpful to join the Facebook groups (e.g. ESN Sevilla, Erasmus-Sevilla ..) and the most helpful are the Whatsapp groups. There you can often get information about apartments, university courses and of course news about all the organized Erasmus events. There is also a buddy program one can join.

Be sure to take warm sweaters and jackets with you !! Even if it has in December and January 15-20 degrees during the day, it gets very cold in the houses, because they are badly isolated. A warm blanket is also necessary at night.

Departure and arrival in Sevilla

In the middle of August I drove towards Seville. After a road trip across France,Northern Spain (beautiful!) And Portugal I arrived in Seville on September 9th. You can read more about it here.

A couple of days before arrival I started searching through several apartment advertisements and sent messages. Unfortunately, as an Erasmus student you rarely get an answer back. All in all, finding an apartment was more difficult than expected. However, I found an advertisement of an Erasmus house on Facebook, which is where I spent the semester with 13 other Erasmus students. In retrospect, however, I would try to find an apartment before arriving in Seville and look at it via Skype , as it saves you a lot of nerves. Also, you shouldn't have too big of expectations when looking for an apartment, as the standards in Seville are different than in Austria for example.

First steps

After I had an apartment, I went to the Centro Internacional (Av. De la Ciudad Jardín, 20). In advance you should organize an appointment online (under cita previa on the website from the US). At the Centro internacional you have to hand in a copy of your passport, ecard copy and passport photo. Have the confirmation of arrival signed and then you must go to the next Santander Bank (at Gran Plaza) to deposit a few euros there. Then you have to go back to the Centro Internacional and show them the confirmation. At the centro nacional you can also register for language courses.

Erasmus Network

Especially at the beginning of the semester, the various Erasmus organizations organize many events. This way you get to know new people very quickly and you can already see a lot of the City. I was mostly traveling with the ESN Sevilla organization. They organized in September a welcome month that was really great. City tours, tapas evenings, pub crawls, Sangriadas, beach trips, picnics, sports, weekend trips and much more.

Spanish

One should know that Sevillian Spanish is very difficult to understand at first. Although I attended a B1 Spanish course beforehand, it was a challenge at first to communicate with my landlords, teachers and other locals. But you get used to it after some time. Do not worry!

Movement

A lot of people use the “Sevici” bike rental system (like the TT bikes in Innsbruck). So I also had an account and was mostly satisfied with it. It can happen that the stations are empty, when you need a bike, or sometimes they are full, when you want to return the bike. If you plan enough time, you can simply drive to the next station. However, especially from 10 am they are a bit full, so you should find out beforehand in the "Sevici" app how many bicycle spaces are available. Friends of mine bought their own bike, and from one of them the bike got stolen. The buses are almost always not on time and take forever due to the many traffic lights. There is only one metro line that goes from west to east, I used this one also very often.

University Life

In general,the university there is very good. A little bit schoolish, with small "classes". In Computer Science if you do all the homeworks during semester you will pass without having to do the final exams. If you want to chill during the semester you can also do nothing and just study for the final exams (risky ;-)). All in all I would say the intensity is similar to Innsbruck.

10 Tips to do during your erasmus exchange in Sevilla

  • Visist the Alhambra in Granada
  • Hike in the Sierra Nevada close to Granada
  • Go surf at the Algarve (Sagres)
  • Hike the Caminito del rey
  • Visit the Pueblos blancos (especially Ronda and Zahara)
  • Book a Surfcam in El Palmar with your friends
  • Chill in the Plaza de Espana and drink a tinto de verano
  • Visit Lisbon with ESN
  • Participate in the welcome month
  • Make many new friends :-)

Of course, Seville itself is beautiful too! Enjoy the time there, the weather and the tapass.
