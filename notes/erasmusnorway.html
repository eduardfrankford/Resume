My Erasmus experience in Oslo, Norway

Posted on July 16, 2021 by Eduard Frankford
Oslo Erasmus

In January 2021 I went to Oslo to study at the Norwegian Business School BI. Therefore, I thought this article might help anyone who is thinking about doing the same. Spoler alert: It was an amazing experience and I can definitely recommend you to do it as well.

Travel preparations:

I went to Norway during the corona pandemic, so the most important thing to get for me was the COVID test. Furthermore, the Norwegian Business School organizes its Erasmus students appartements in advance, so I accepted the housing offer of Anker Studentbolig and paid the first month of rent plus the deposit. The list of different housing providers can be found here.

Furthermore, always important when you go on Erasmus is the "Learning Agreement", which is a list of the courses you can register for in the foreign university and then get credits for those in your home university. For the Norwegian Business School they have a separate list of courses specially for exchange students. The list can be found here.

As you probably already know Norway can be pretty cold. Therefore, don't forget warm clothes. Especially, if you are going there in winter.

Another thing I wish I took with me for my exchange, is all my ski equipment, because ski touring equipment is very expensive to rent (around 50 euros a day). If I would have take my ski touring equipment, I could have used it as well to go skiing at Skimore, which is the ski resort close to Oslo.
If you like to go skating (Cross country skiing) then I would also recommend to you to take your equipment with you, because it is very hard to find skating ski to rent. However, if you like the classic style then you can leave your equipment at home and rent something for the whole season very cheaply or buy something on finn, which is the Norwegian market for used goods

For the surfers here I bought a Surfboard there as well and then went surfing during the easter break in Stavanger and afterwards I sold it again on Finn with a little profit. :-P

Departure and arrival at the place of study

I flew to Oslo from Vienna. Once arrived at the Oslo Airport it is really easy to get from the Airport to the city center by train. Once you are at the airport just ask an airport employee and they will show you the stairs to the railway station. It is possible to buy the tickets at a ticket machine or online with the Ruter Billet app. After having arrived at the central station I had to go to the quarantine hotel, which was also paid by the Norwegian Business School BI. After the quarantine I moved in to my appartement.

Anker Studentbolig

I lived at the Anker Studentbolig because I got an offer from them through the business school. I have to say that my stay there was not good but also not bad.
The advantages are:

  • It is very central
  • It is relatively cheap
  • You are surrounded by other erasmus students
  • If you are at a high floor you have a very good view

The disadvantages are:

  • Nearly every party got stopped at 11 pm because of somebody calling the security
  • They charge for every small extra service (once I locked myself out and called the security to open the door -> 50 Euros)
  • It can be a little noisy (So take your airpods with you ;-))

Erasmus Network

When I was there the Erasmus Student Organisations organized nearly no events. Maybe because it was Covid time or maybe because they don't exist. But I created a whatsapp group and posted the link in an Erasmus group on Facebook. Over this link nearly 150 people joined the group and then wrote a message to ask if people want to go hiking, skiing, drinking etc.

Language

In Norway nearly everybody speaks English perfectly. Therefore, it is not neccessary to learn Norwegian, unless you are very motivated do it. The Norwegian language is very similar to German, so depending on wherer you come from it might be easy or hard for you to learn it. All in all, it might be a plus to speak Norwegian, because it allows you to make Norwegian friends easily.

Movement

I bought the ruter monthly ticket for around 400 NOK per month. You can also additionally buy the city bycicle subscription because it is very cheap.

BI Campus

The BI Campus is one of the most beautiful campuses I have ever seen. It is very modern and open and also has a very good and cheap cafeteria.

10 Things I would recommend to do in Norway during exchange

  • Rent cabins together with friends
  • Go surfing in Stavanger or the Lofoten (Unstad)
  • Go to Tromsoe and see the Northern Lights
  • Go ski touring or hiking in the Lofoten
  • Go cross country skiing in Oslo
  • Go ice bathing at Akker Brygge
  • Go play table-tennis at Akker Brygge
  • Go workout at Akker Brygge
  • Go to Sognsvann with friends and make a bonfire
  • Spent the 17th of May (National Day) in Bergen

I hope that I could help you with this article and enjoy your stay in Norways!
