My Erasmus experience in Oslo, Norway

Posted on July 16, 2021 by Eduard Frankford
Oslo Erasmus

Travel preparations:

I went to Norway during the corona pandemic, so the most important thing to get for me was the COVID test. Furthermore, the Norwegian Business School organizes its Erasmus students appartements in advance, so I accepted the housing offer of Anker Studentbolig and paid the first month of rent plus the deposit.

Departure and arrival at the place of study

I flew to Oslo from Vienna. Once arrived at the Oslo Airport it is really easy to get from the Airport to the city center by train. Once you are there just ask a local / employee and they will show you the stairs to the railway station. It is possible to buy the tickets at a machine or online with the Ruter Billet app. After having arrived at the central station I had to go to the quarantine hotel which was also paid by the BI. After the quarantine I moved in to my appartement.

Anker Studentbolig

I lived at the Anker Studentbolig because I got an offer from them through the business school. I have to say that my stay there was not good but also not bad.
The advantages are:

  • It is very central
  • It is relatively cheap
  • You are surrounded by other erasmus students
  • If you are at a high floor you have a good view

The disadvantages are:

  • Nearly every party got stopped at 11 pm because of somebody calling the security
  • They charge for every small extra service (once I locked myself out and called the security to open the door -> 50 Euros)
  • It can be a little noisy

Erasmus Network

When I was there the Erasmus Student Organisations organized nearly no events. Maybe because it was Covid time or maybe because they don't exist. But I created a whatsapp group and posted the link in an Erasmus Facebook group. Over this link nearly 150 people joined the group and then wrote a message to ask if people want to go hiking, skiing, drinking etc.

Language

In Norway nearly everybody speaks English perfectly. Therefor, it is not neccessary to learn Norwegian, but if you are very motivated it is a nice plus if you can speak it and then it might be easier to join Norwegian parties. :-)

Movement

I bought the Ruter monthly ticket for around 400 NOK per month. You can also additionally buy the city bicicle subscription because it is very cheap.

BI Campus

The BI Campus is one of the most beautiful campuses I have ever seen. It is very modern and open and also has a very good and cheap cafeteria.

Tipps for outgoing students

Don't forget your warm clothes and if you are motivated to do outdoor sports take you ski equipment with you because especially touring skis are very expensive to rent. Cross country skis on the other hand are cheap to rent no need to take them with you. I rented them at BOA for around 70 euros for the whole season.

Things I would recommend to do

  • Rent cabins together with friends
  • Go surfing in Stavanger or the Lofoten
  • Go to Tromsoe and see the Northern Lights
  • Go ski touring or hiking in the Lofoten
  • Go cross country skiing in Oslo
  • Go ice bathing at Akker Brygge
  • Go to Sognsvann with friends and make a bonfire
Enjoy your stay!
