Roadtrip from Innsbruck to Sevilla

Posted on July 27, 2021 by Eduard Frankford and Sophia Thummer
Roadtrip 2019

Montreux at the lake of Genf

A car, a tent and a surfboard. That and a whole lot of clothes was all we needed when my girlfriend and I were leaving for what would turn out to be one of the best experiences of our lives. In the middle of August 2019 we left Austria with the final destination Seville, where we would do 6 months of Erasmus. Our first stop was Montreux, a beautiful town at the lake of Genf. We found a wonderful camping site directly at the lake, which was relatively cheap, considering swiss standards. There, we enjoyed the clear water and the beautiful view over the Alps until it started raining heavily a day later. Because of all the rain, we decided to head to the western coast of France as fast as we can.

Perigeux the city of wine

6 hours by car later we were in sunny Perigeux, a small medieval town with delicious wine. We rented an AirBnb there because our tent was still soaking wet. In a desperate intent to make it dry again, we put it over our car and probably looked like homeless people at that point. Luckily the next day the tent was dry again (and our car extremely dirty :-D).

Mimizan

After that we headed west to Mimizan, a place on the western coast of France, known for good waves and what we didn’t know: kind of a weird smell. On the first day we were wondering why the camping space or one of us was smelling. We joked about our neighbors or about how we should shower more often, until we talked to some locals who told us that a trash factory was nearby and when the wind was offshore in the morning, the smell of trash spreads through the whole town. A thing to consider when one comes to Mimizan. Besides the funny smell, we spent some nice days at the camping space, studying Spanish and paddling into our first waves.

Zarautz

Our next stop was Zarautz, a beautiful spot with one of the greatest sunsets. Since the camping space was full when we arrived, we had to improvise and put our tent into a field next to many other tents. Luckily a German guy was so kind to let us connect to his electricity so we had cool beer again. We enjoyed the great view over the town and went the 1000 steps down to the beach and then up again (great views come with a price).

Galicia

After Zarautz, we made our way through many small and beautiful places in northern Spain, even joined a surf-camp for a week in Galicia. The surf-camp was newly founded by two nice Germans and was for intermediate surfers, which was hard at first, because there were some pretty high waves to surf, but I loved it. Galicia was cheap and a car is a must have there, since there are not so many buses. From the surf-camp in Valdovino we drove a few times to Pantin, a very famous beach where the Pantin calssics (a surf tournament) was being held at the time.

Peniche

Before we got to Seville, we made one last stop in Peniche, Portugal. Peniche is a nice fishing village with many surf-spots, especially famous for the super tubos beach, which offers big barrels to surf when the conditions are right.

Sevilla:

Although we had some great days in Peniche, we grew more nervous the closer we got to Seville, since we didn’t have any accommodation or confirmed courses for university in advance. So when we reached Seville on September 9th, we were relieved to find a accommodation in an Erasmus house on Facebook on the same day, which is where we spent the semester with 13 other Erasmus students.

After we got accommodated, we went to organize our courses for university. Well, ORGANIZE is maybe exaggerated, since everything in Seville was exactly the opposite of organized. Buses never came when they should, shops were closed when you needed them and secretarys in the university ALWAYS empty. It was a struggle for sure to get all the courses we needed. But in the end everything worked out pretty good, considering our Spanish was not not the best at the beginning. What nobody told us, is that the “Sevillianos” have a very strong accent as well, so as you can imagine, the first weeks we had the one or other difficulty communicating. But, because all of our courses in were in Spanish, we improved a lot and can now claim to speak Spanish pretty fluently. Especially at the beginning of the semester we joined many events of ESN (Erasmus network Seville). They organized a welcome month that was really great. City tours, tapas evenings, pub crawls, Sangriadas, beach trips, picnics, sports, weekend trips and much more.

We had a lot of fun and met many people from all over the world. After a short time, we found ourselves in a great group with around 10 people, with whom we spent most of our free time in Seville. I couldn’t even count how many tapas I ate, Tintos de Verano (red wine with Fanta lemon – delicious) I drank and went out to party at clubs. On the weekends we did a lot of trips. I have been to Cordoba, Valencia, Granada, the “pueblos blancos”, the Algarve, Cadíz, El Palmar (where you can surf in winter) and at the Caminito del rey. So the months went by very fast. University went well for me, I had a great time with my friends, visited beautiful places and felt very connected to Seville and the Spanish culture. When I had to leave Seville at the end of January, I was a little sad in fact. Those 6 months were awesome and I would do it all over again. But at the same time, I appreciated coming home to Austria and being back in the mountains again.
