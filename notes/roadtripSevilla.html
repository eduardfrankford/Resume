Roadtrip from Innsbruck to Sevilla

Posted on July 27, 2021 by Eduard Frankford and Sophia Thummer
Roadtrip 2019

A car, a tent and a surfboard. That and a whole lot of clothes was all we needed when my girlfriend and I were leaving for what would turn out to be one of the best experiences of our lives. In the middle of August 2019 we left Austria with the final destination Seville, where we would do 6 months of Erasmus.

Montreux at the lake of Genf

Our first stop was Montreux, a beautiful town at the lake of Genf. We found a wonderful camping site directly at the lake, which was relatively cheap, considering Swiss standards. The camping site is we stayed at can be found here
In montreux, we enjoyed the clear water of the lake geneve and the beautiful view over the Alps. Until, it started raining heavily a day later, because of all the rain we decided to continue our journey and head to the western coast of France as fast as we can.

Perigeux the city of wine

6 hours by car later we were in sunny Perigeux, a small medieval town with delicious wine. We rented an AirBnb there because our tent was still soaking wet. In a desperate intent to make it dry again, we put it over our car and probably looked like homeless people at that point. Luckily, the next day the tent was dry again (and our car extremely dirty :-D).

Mimizan

After that we headed west to Mimizan, a place on the western coast of France, known for good waves and what we didn't know: kind of a weird smell. On the first day we were wondering whether the camping space or one of us was smelling. We joked about our neighbors or about how we should shower more often. Some days later, we talked to some locals who told us that a trash factory was nearby and when the wind was offshore in the morning, the smell of trash spreads through the whole town. A thing to consider when one comes to Mimizan. Besides the funny smell, we spent some nice days at the camping space, studying Spanish and paddling into our first waves.

Zarautz

Our next stop was Zarautz, a beautiful spot with one of the greatest sunsets. Since the camping space was full when we arrived, we had to improvise and put our tent into a field next to many other tents. Luckily a German guy was so kind to let us connect to his electricity so we had cool beer again. The camping was called TODO and the website can be found here. The camping in Zarautz has one of the best views we have had during our trip. However to get to the town you need to take approximately 1000 steps down, but all in all it is definitely worth it.

Galicia

After Zarautz, we made our way through many small and beautiful places in northern Spain, even joined a surf-camp for a week in Galicia. The surf-camp was newly founded by two nice Germans and was for intermediate surfers. The surf-camp is called Laybak Surfcamp and can be found here. Sophia struggeled a bit ,because there were some pretty high waves to surf, but I loved it. Galicia was cheap and a car is a must have there, since there are not so many buses. From the surf-camp in Valdovino we drove a few times to Pantin, a very famous beach where the Pantin calssics (a surf tournament) were being held at the time.

Peniche

Before we got to Seville, we made one last stop in Peniche, Portugal. Peniche is a nice fishing village with many surf-spots, especially famous for the super tubos beach, which offers big barrels to surf when the conditions are right.

Sevilla:

Although, we had some great days in Peniche, we grew more nervous the closer we got to Seville, because in once we arrived the road trip would be over and the next adventure would start, Erasmus in Sevilla (Read here).

In Sevilla we had a lot of fun and met many people from all over the world. After a short time, we found ourselves in a great group with around 10 people, with whom we spent most of our free time in Seville. I couldn't even count how many tapas I ate, Tintos de Verano (red wine with Fanta lemon -> delicious) I drank and went out to party at clubs. From Sevilla you can do many trips. I have been to Cordoba, Valencia, Granada, the “pueblos blancos”, the Algarve, Cadíz, El Palmar (where you can surf in winter) and did the Caminito del rey. All in all, I had a great time with my friends, visited beautiful places and felt very connected to Seville and the Spanish culture. I hope you good some valuable information out of this article and that you will enjoy the places recommended as much as I did!
